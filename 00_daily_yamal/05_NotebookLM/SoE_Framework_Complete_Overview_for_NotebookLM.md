## 1. What Is SoE?

SoE stands for two things simultaneously:

- **Service of Empowerment**: a system that maximises the capability of people receiving support
- **Service or Exploitation**: a diagnostic tool that determines whether a service actually empowers or structurally exploits

The dual meaning is intentional. The constructive layer (Empowerment) describes what the system builds. The critical layer (Exploitation) describes what it detects and prevents.

**Core motto:** "Every service must prove itself not to exploit."

SoE was developed by a Japanese practitioner-researcher (Researcher Y) with over 10 years of direct practice experience in disability employment support, building on 15 years of theoretical continuity beginning with PHR Commons (2011) — a personal health record democratisation project that established Researcher Y's core conviction: information sovereignty belongs to the individual, not the institution. The practice field is specifically vocational transition support (就労移行支援), where the goal is helping people with disabilities find and sustain employment within a two-year programme.

### The Ethical Equation

At its simplest, SoE reduces to one formula:

```
Service Quality = Empowerment Index − Exploitation Index
```

**Empowerment indicators**: expansion of choices, growth in self-esteem and self-efficacy, horizontal support relationship (co-agency), participation in decision-making, reasonable accommodation provided.

**Exploitation indicators**: coercion, information asymmetry, unilateral benefit extraction, damage to self-esteem, exclusion, dependency creation, support data reused without consent, restricted choices, unpaid labour, infantilisation, schema-driven "choice" at low self-esteem (autonomic survival responses misidentified as voluntary decisions), psychological bondage masked as compliance.

Evaluation uses a triple system: AI review + human professional review + person-centred feedback from the individual themselves.

## 2. Philosophical Foundations

### 2.1 The Double-Entry Bookkeeping Metaphor for Care

This is the central metaphor of the entire framework. Every person's life is modelled as a set of financial statements — just like a corporation.

#### The Three Financial Statements

| Statement | Cognitive Mode | What It Tracks |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Balance Sheet (BS)** | Deductive — "Who should I be?" | **Assets**: basic human rights, dignity, education, skills, relationships, self-esteem. **Liabilities**: trauma, stigma, social barriers, cognitive distortions |
| **Profit & Loss (PL)** | Inductive — "How does society treat me?" | **Revenue**: positive experiences, skill acquisition, recognition. **Expenses**: failure experiences, discrimination, survival costs |
| **Cash Flow (CF)** | Behavioural capacity | Self-esteem, self-efficacy, physical and mental energy — the actual "cash" available for daily action |

#### Key Audit Points

- **BS Audit**: "Self-negation — believing one is worthless — is an accounting fraud. It means human rights (assets) have been omitted from the books."
- **PL Audit**: "Is over-adaptation — working too hard to appear functional — being achieved by depleting the mental health balance sheet? Surface-level profit at the cost of capital destruction."
- **CF Audit**: "Is the person's daily energy being consumed entirely by managing unresolved trauma? Zero cash flow despite balance sheet assets?"

#### Accounting Metaphor Components in Detail

**Human Rights as Capital**: Every person is born with equal capital — their human rights. This is non-negotiable and equal for all.

From this capital, specific rights emerge:
- **Intangible Fixed Assets**: Rights like education, healthcare, employment — invisible but enormously valuable
- **Deferred Assets**: Family environment — an investment that should gradually convert into capabilities through "normal amortisation." Abuse and neglect cause "abnormal amortisation," destroying value
- **Tangible Fixed Assets**: Physical and cognitive abilities
- **Current Assets**: Self-esteem (the most liquid asset — like cash), self-efficacy (accounts receivable), resilience (inventory)

**Liabilities:**
- **Fixed Liabilities**: ACEs (long-term loans), early maladaptive schemas (deep structural debt), internalised stigma
- **Current Liabilities**: Depression/anxiety (accounts payable), cognitive distortions (accrued expenses), environmental barriers

**Net Worth** = Assets minus Liabilities = the person's real capacity to participate in society.

### 2.2 Tobashi: Off-Balance-Sheet Fraud in Welfare

"Tobashi" (飛ばし) is a Japanese corporate accounting term meaning "to make losses disappear" by moving them off the balance sheet — the same technique used in the Enron scandal and in numerous Japanese corporate frauds (Olympus, Toshiba).

SoE argues that the welfare system performs a **structural tobashi**:

**Step 1**: Society (the "parent company") has an obligation to provide education, healthcare, and social support. This obligation costs money. Society fails to pay this cost — particularly for children with adverse childhood experiences.

**Step 2**: The resulting deficiency in the individual (the "subsidiary") — low self-esteem, poor vocational readiness, behavioural difficulties — is then recorded as the individual's own "lack of ability" or "irresponsibility." The social debt is transferred onto the individual's balance sheet.

**Step 3**: Society's Profit & Loss statement looks clean — graduation rates are maintained, standardised test scores are adequate, employment statistics show progress. But the hidden liability — the unpaid cost of properly supporting traumatised and disabled individuals — has simply been made invisible.

**The SoE Intervention: Forced Correction Entry** (強制修正仕訳)

When SoE detects a tobashi, it mandates:
1. Transfer the individual's liabilities (their "hardship of living") back to society's balance sheet as an "extraordinary loss"
2. Demand immediate capital injection — the resources, support, and accommodation that should have been provided from the beginning

#### The Education Example

Japan's Fundamental Law of Education establishes a **process obligation** for personality formation and human development. But in practice:
- Schools graduate students without building emotional foundations
- The diploma exists on the books, but the human development it was supposed to represent never occurred
- This is an **impaired asset** — a fixed asset with unrecognised losses
- The impairment is never written down; instead, it becomes an "off-balance-sheet loss transferred to the individual's consolidated accounts"

## 3. Social Theory Layer

### 3.1 The Bipolar Social Damage Model

Society is damaged from both extremes simultaneously:

| Factor | Direction | Mechanism | Trigger |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Anomie Factor** (アノミー因子) | Bottom-up destruction | Loss of motivation to follow the rule of law | Absolute deprivation of rights — nothing left to lose |
| **Hypnos Factor** (ヒュプノス因子) | Top-down commodification | Structural cognitive dormancy — "sleeping" through injustice | Extreme concentration of rights and resources |

#### The Anomie Factor

Named after Durkheim and Merton's concept of normlessness. When a person is systematically stripped of rights, resources, and social connection, they reach a threshold where the social contract no longer applies to them — because it never protected them. This is the "muteki-no-hito" (無敵の人) phenomenon in Japanese discourse: a person with nothing to lose.

In accounting terms: society's long-term unpaid debt, not a personality defect. The treatment is capital injection (rights restoration), not punishment.

#### The Hypnos Factor

Named after the Greek god of sleep — deliberately NOT "Hubris" (arrogance). The distinction is crucial:

| | Hubris | Hypnos |
|---|---|---|
| Meaning | Arrogance — a moral attribution | Sleep — a structural, probabilistic phenomenon |
| Story | "A bad person became arrogant" | "Anyone can fall asleep under these structural conditions" |
| Intervention | Punish/educate the individual | Change the structure |
| Explanatory power | One person's moral failure | Why MIT, the judiciary, and the media "slept" for 20 years while Epstein operated |

**Properties of the Hypnos Factor:**
- **Structural, not moral**: Can occur even in "good people" — Gates, Clinton, and Prince Andrew all have daughters, yet the recognition circuits shut down
- **Threshold-dependent**: Below a certain level of power/resource concentration, it does not manifest; above the threshold, it becomes probabilistic
- **Scale-invariant**: Micro-exploitation in a welfare office and macro-exploitation (Epstein) are the same factor operating at different scales
- **Affects institutions**: Organisations collectively "fall asleep" — their self-cleansing and whistleblower functions go dormant

**What Hypnos puts to sleep:**
1. Legal sensibility — the inner commitment to equality under law
2. Moral recognition circuits — "the person opposite me is also a human being"
3. Institutional immunity — organisational self-correction mechanisms

### 3.2 The Abuse Infection Model

Abuse operates like an infectious disease — it spreads through contact and can be transmitted across generations.

**The Infection Cycle:**
1. Being victimised → self-esteem drops ("I am worthless")
2. Harming someone weaker → "I have power too" → self-esteem temporarily rises
3. The harm "succeeds" (no consequences) → "I won't get caught"
4. Repetition → "I am exceptional" → Hypnos Factor onset

**Intergenerational transmission**: Debt is inherited. A sibling commits a crime → the family is labelled → a sister's marriage breaks down → her children are affected. The "liability" passes through generations like a revolving line of credit that nobody asked for.

**SoE Connection**: Rights imbalance is the true pathogen — it precedes wealth inequality. Anomie and Hypnos are both symptoms of the same underlying disease. SoE's Tobashi Detection exposes when an individual is being blamed for debts that were actually society's, enabling intervention before the infection cycle begins.

### 3.3 Quasi-Mandate Contract Theory

The legal foundation of the supporter-person relationship in Japanese welfare:

**Civil Code Article 656** defines quasi-mandate contracts (準委任契約) — delegation of non-legal affairs with a **duty of care as a competent manager** (善管注意義務).

Key distinction: this is a **process-debt**, not a result-debt. A doctor is obligated to try their best, not to guarantee a cure. A welfare supporter is obligated to provide appropriate, diligent support — not to guarantee employment outcomes.

**Three Phases of Welfare Service Contract:**

| Phase | Period | Structure | Problem |
|-------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Phase 1 | Pre-2000 (Measures System) | Administrative decides unilaterally | No choice for the person; pure paternalism |
| Phase 2 | 2000-present (Contract System) | Person and provider sign contract | Contract content is vague; quasi-mandate nature unrecognised |
| Phase 3 | SoE Proposal | Quasi-mandate clarified + Constitutional AI | Record sovereignty + ethical traceability + measurable duty of care |

**SoE's Proposed Reform**: The Individual Support Plan should be explicitly recognised as a quasi-mandate contract document. The content of 善管注意義務 becomes concrete and measurable — for example: "Raise the person's self-esteem from below the 50th percentile to above the 50th percentile within approximately 2 months, as measured by NOCC assessment."

Applying Ingrid Robeyns' theory of upper limits on wealth accumulation to the welfare context. The question is not only "do people have enough?" (sufficientarianism) but also "does anyone have too much power over others?"

In welfare systems, power concentration creates the same Hypnos-inducing dynamics as wealth concentration: service providers, administrators, and institutions accumulate unchecked power over the people they are supposed to serve.

**Input Constitutional AI as Limitarianism's implementation device**: SoE does not rely on moral appeals to limit welfare power. This is not surveillance of workers; it is an architectural constraint on institutional power, analogous to how constitutional separation of powers constrains state authority.

**Proof of Concept**: MySupport.yaml data from Japanese B-type sheltered workshops — where monthly wages average ~16,000 yen and general employment transition rates hover near 1% — provides measurable empirical evidence for Robeyns' theoretical framework. The data demonstrates that welfare power concentration is not hypothetical: it produces quantifiable exploitation signatures that can be detected, measured, and structurally prevented. SoE transforms Robeyns from political philosophy into operational audit technology.

### 4.1 Input Constitutional AI

This is SoE's most significant technical innovation — and arguably a world first.

**The Discovery** (October 2025): During a literature review on Anthropic's Constitutional AI, a gap was identified. ALL existing Constitutional AI research focuses on the **Output** side — filtering or aligning what AI systems produce after generation. Nobody had addressed the **Input** side: what happens when data is collected and records are created.

| Dimension | Output CAI (Existing) | Input CAI (SoE Innovation) |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Focus | Post-hoc check of generated content | Rights guarantee at the moment of record creation |
| Purpose | Remove harmfulness from AI output | Prevent rights violations from entering the database |
| Target | AI model training and inference | Human record-creation process, guided by AI |
| Scope | All existing CAI research | No prior research exists (world first) |

**How It Works:**
1. A support worker attempts to create a record about a person
2. The system checks the record against constitutional principles (CRPD + quasi-mandate contract theory)
3. Questions are raised: "Does this record guarantee the person's rights?" "Can the person access their own record?" "Is this attributing a social failure to the individual?"
4. Only rights-compliant records enter the database

**Input CAI as Forensic Audit Technology**: Input CAI is not merely an ethical filter. It is a forensic auditing system for consolidated accounts — detecting where society (the parent company) has transferred its unpaid obligations onto the individual (the subsidiary) and hidden them off the balance sheet. This is the same logic used to detect corporate fraud in Enron, Olympus, and Toshiba — applied to welfare.

When the Exploitation Index exceeds a defined threshold, the system triggers specific enforcement actions:
- **Alert**: The record is flagged and the support worker is prompted to reconsider the framing ("This record attributes a structural failure to the individual — is this accurate?")
- **Record rejection**: If the record attributes social debts to the individual without evidence (e.g. "lazy and irresponsible" for someone with processing speed at the 0.2 percentile), the system blocks entry until the framing is corrected
- **Forced Correction Entry**: The system generates a counter-entry that records the structural context — what environmental factors, institutional failures, or rights violations contributed to the person's current state
- **Audit trail**: Every flag, rejection, and correction is logged — creating an institutional accountability record that cannot be erased

This transforms Input CAI from a passive checkpoint into an active forensic tool that makes institutional tobashi structurally impossible.

**Empirical Evidence of Output CAI's Limitations**: When Claude (Anthropic's AI) was given a rescue operation record involving confinement and exploitation, the safety system auto-triggered a crisis helpline message — "If you or someone you know is going through a difficult time..." — misidentifying the support professional (the rescuer) as someone in crisis. Output CAI works by keyword matching without semantic context. It could potentially filter out records of abuse evidence before problems are captured. This is "tobashi in reverse" — the AI system making problems invisible rather than visible.

### 4.3 Record Sovereignty (記録主権)

The right of the person receiving support to own and control their own support records.

**Five Components:**
1. **Ownership**: Records belong to the person, not the service provider
2. **Access**: Free access at any time — no gatekeeping
3. **Control**: The person decides what to disclose and to whom
4. **Modification**: Right to object to and correct inaccurate records
5. **Portability**: Ability to migrate records to another system or provider

**Legal Foundation:**
- Civil Code Article 656: Records created under quasi-mandate contract are for the benefit of the person and should be owned by the person
- Personal Information Protection Act: Existing rights to disclosure, correction, and suspension — SoE advances these from "data held by businesses" to "direct ownership by the person"
- CRPD Article 12 (equal recognition of legal capacity), Article 21 (freedom of expression and access to information)

## 5. Implementation Framework

### 5.1 The Four-Pillar Approach

These are not four separate research areas — they are the four structural supports of the single SoE system.

#### Pillar 1: Rights Recovery (権利回復)

**Role**: Why SoE is necessary — the location of the problem

**Evidence**: NOCC data from 10 years of practice shows that self-esteem of people with disabilities in employment support averages the 10th-20th percentile of the general population. This is not a personal failing — it is the statistical signature of systematic under-investment.

### 5.3 Grain Engineering (粒度工学)

The design discipline that determines at what granularity level observation, recording, and intervention should occur within SoE. Complexity theory tells us that welfare problems exist across Simple, Complicated, and Complex domains — but it does not tell us at what resolution to observe. This is SoE's core technical contribution: **designing the grain**.

#### Three-Tier Grain Model

| Tier | Level | Who Operates | What It Covers |
|------|-------|--------------|----------------|
| **Tier 1: Macro** | Standard grain | Top 30% of supporters ("Nice Layer") | Self-esteem score tracking, basic literacy/numeracy, decision-making choice presentation, meeting log summaries |
| **Tier 2: Meso** | Mid-grain | Advanced SoE practitioners | Early warning signs of self-esteem collapse, detection of structural aftereffects of critical life decisions, supporter cognitive bias analysis, unarticulated client needs |
| **Tier 3: Micro** | Expert grain | Specialist researchers | Distributed representation modelling, non-verbal information analysis, micro-differential curves of self-esteem recovery, social disruption factor decomposition |

**Design goal**: The LLM is not a microscope (replacing the expert). It is common eyeglasses — making Tier 1 grain visible and operable for the Nice Layer. Tier 3 remains the domain of expert practitioners; the system's job is to translate Tier 3 insights downward into Tier 1 tools.

**Required properties of engineered grain**: reproducible, understandable by the person themselves, operable by the top 30% of supporters, processable by LLM in structured format, and self-revealing of quality through the Record Exposure Model.

#### The Record Exposure Model (記録露光モデル)

A governance innovation that eliminates the need for top-down evaluation of support workers:

**Core principle**: "There is no need to evaluate supporters. Records expose everything."

The granularity of a support worker's records naturally reveals their quality — the depth of dialogue logs, the number of choices presented to the person, how the person's own narrative is handled, the presence or absence of contextual evidence. Good practice floats to the surface; coarse practice sinks — automatically, without surveillance.

**SoE-AI functions not as a judge but as a "developer" (photographic exposure device)** — it develops what is already latent in the records.

### 5.4 The 30% Strategy (ナイス層戦略)

The deployment strategy for SoE technology — and a fundamental rejection of the welfare sector's assumption that every method should be universally teachable.

**Core principle**: "You can teach technology to nice people, but you cannot teach niceness to technically skilled people."

SoE is **selective technology**, not standard technology. It targets the top 30% of support workers — those with high empathy, ethical sensitivity, dialogue resilience, and capacity to absorb new frameworks ("the Nice Layer"). The remaining 70% benefit indirectly through the Record Exposure Model, which naturally elevates the standard of care without requiring every individual to master the system.

**Implementation:**
- Training is limited to the top 30%
- Records are shared universally, but grain-level operation is the 30%'s responsibility
- LLM is designed as "eyeglasses for the Nice 30%" — not a microscope for everyone
- Giving sophisticated technology to non-Nice supporters risks misuse

**Expected effect**: Non-linear quality improvement across the entire welfare community — because the 30% sets the standard that records naturally reveal, and the institutional culture shifts upward.

### 5.5 The Co-LLM Session Protocol

A three-way session structure: Expert (human professional) + LLM (large language model) + Person (client).

| Configuration | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| LLM alone with person | Psychological safety, 24h availability, empathy-rich | Bias toward affirmation, may enable dependency |
| Expert alone with person | Structural perspective, can challenge, conveys reality | Expert viewpoint "hits directly," triggers defensiveness |
| **Three-way (SoE model)** | LLM buffers expert insight; person receives challenge without direct hit | Setup time; requires expert monitoring |

**Role Distribution:**
- **Expert**: Identifies structural risks, challenges LLM's tendency to affirm, provides "auxiliary lines" (guiding insights)
- **LLM**: Not a microscope (expert replacement) but a granularity exposure device — translates between emotion and structure, adjusts vocabulary, organises options, serves as psychological safety buffer. In Grain Engineering terms: converts Tier 3 expert insights into Tier 1 language, functions as "eyeglasses for the Nice 30%," and operates as a record structure visualisation tool
- **Person**: Focuses on verbalising their own perception; receives buffered insights in language they can process

**Schema Building Protocol**: Before the three-way session, the LLM conducts 3-5 pre-sessions with the person to build a YAML schema of their cognitive and linguistic characteristics — vocabulary level, emotional triggers, preferred explanation style, decision-making patterns. This schema is loaded at the start of the joint session.

**SoE Connection**: In traditional assessment, the supporter "measures" the client, who is the object. In Schema Building Assessment, the person is the co-author of their own assessment.

### 5.7 Two-Axis Organisational Mission Model

For welfare organisations implementing SoE:

| Axis | Purpose | Key Principle |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Axis 1: Business Execution | Organisational sustainability | Complex → Complicated → Simple cycle |
| Axis 2: Rehabilitation Function | Person empowerment | Assessment of behavioural challenges related to "earning," not disability traits |

Both axes concern "earning" — the organisation earning revenue (Axis 1) and the person becoming able to earn a livelihood (Axis 2). ### 5.8 Information Boundary Design

SoE proposes a tripartite information architecture — three stakeholders, each with different information access:

| Stakeholder | What They See | What They Don't See |
|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **The Person** | PL/BS/CF — full self-understanding of past → present; supports disability acceptance | N/A — full access |
| **The Supporter** | Clinical and support-relevant data; SoE scores; progress tracking | Detail is governed by person's consent |
| **The Organisation (Employer)** | Only "predicted future difficulties and necessary reasonable accommodations" | No past diagnostic data; no ACE scores; no psychological assessments; no schema information (YSQ); no autonomic state data (HRV) |

**Minimal Necessary Disclosure Principle**: Information flows upward only to the extent necessary for the stated purpose. The person controls the gates. This is CRPD-compliant by design.

**Employer boundary clarification**: The employer sees ONLY what is needed for reasonable accommodation — never the underlying reasons. Schema information (which early maladaptive schemas are active) and ACE data (what happened in childhood) are categorically excluded from employer disclosure. This design is grounded in CRPD Article 12 (equal recognition of legal capacity) — the person's psychological history is not the employer's business; their functional needs in the workplace are.

## 6. The Hypnos Factor: Extended Analysis

The Hypnos Factor deserves extended treatment because it represents SoE's most original contribution to social theory.

### Historical Pattern: 2,000 Years of the Same Structure

From China's First Emperor (Qin Shi Huang, 3rd century BCE) through the Roman emperors, through the European aristocracy, through the Gilded Age robber barons, through Jeffrey Epstein — the pattern has not changed:

1. Extreme concentration of power/resources in an individual or small group
2. The concentrated power exceeds the threshold where normal moral recognition circuits function
3. Other people become instruments — means to the power-holder's ends
4. Institutions that should check this power "fall asleep" (judiciary, media, academia, civil society)
5. The system persists until external shock breaks it

The tools have been refined over 2,000 years — from armies to financial instruments to technology platforms — but the underlying structure has not changed by a millimetre.

### Scale Invariance: From Welfare Office to Epstein Island

The same Hypnos Factor operates at every scale:

- **Micro**: A welfare service provider who has unchecked power over vulnerable people and gradually begins to treat them as administrative units rather than human beings
- **Meso**: A corporation that employs people with disabilities for the subsidy while providing no meaningful support or development
- **Macro**: A billionaire who uses wealth to commodify human beings while institutions look away

The mechanism is identical. Only the magnitude differs. This is why SoE insists on structural intervention (changing the conditions that produce Hypnos) rather than moral intervention (telling individuals to be better).

### Why "Sleep" and Not "Evil"

The Hypnos framing is deliberate and important:

1. **It removes moral blame from the analysis**. This is not about good people versus bad people. It is about structural conditions that produce predictable cognitive changes.
2. **It makes the phenomenon investigable**. "Evil" is not a research variable. "Cognitive dormancy under conditions of power asymmetry" is.
3. **It enables prevention**. You cannot prevent evil. You can design structures that prevent the conditions under which cognitive dormancy occurs.
4. **It explains institutional failure**. "Evil" cannot explain why MIT accepted Epstein's money for 20 years. "Collective sleep" — structural cognitive dormancy affecting an entire institution — can.

## 7. Practical Evidence: What the Data Shows

### Case Evidence

Over 10 years of practice across multiple disability employment support facilities, Researcher Y accumulated evidence showing:

- Self-esteem of people entering employment support averaged the 10th-20th percentile of the general population
- Automated assessment systems generated descriptions like "lazy, lacking perseverance, and irresponsible" for people whose processing speed was at the 0.2 percentile
- The gap between automated assessment and observed reality (through case notes, chat logs, and behavioural tracking) consistently showed that individual "deficits" were actually unrecorded social debts
- People who received intensive, rights-based support achieved outcomes (certifications, employment, relationship reconstruction) that directly contradicted their assessment profiles

### The Support Worker's Balance Sheet

One of SoE's most uncomfortable insights: the accumulated debt of education, medicine, and family is ultimately absorbed by individual support workers. When these workers' personal balance sheets break — when the invisible liabilities exceed what one person can carry — the support system collapses.

The question that SoE raises but the welfare system has never answered: **Who holds the duty of care for the person providing duty of care?**

### The Chat-Based Support System (Proto-Constitutional AI)

Before large language models existed, Researcher Y designed a system that anticipated many Input CAI principles:

- **Text, not phone calls**: Every crisis interaction generated an automatic record
- **Shared space**: All conversations visible to person, supporter, and team — transparency by design
- **Time boundaries**: Clear scope of duty of care (support available until 9pm)
- **Daily tracking**: Continuous quantitative record alongside qualitative case notes

Every element maps to what an LLM could automate: first-response triage, pattern detection, risk flagging, and structured documentation. The 2022 manual system is the prototype for the 2025+ AI-enhanced system.

## 8. International Context

### IPS (Individual Placement and Support)

The US evidence-based model for competitive employment for people with severe mental illness. Core principle: "Anyone who wants to work can work, given adequate support." SoE aligns with IPS philosophy but adds the accounting metaphor and Constitutional AI layer.

### Samhall (Sweden)

State-owned supported employment company. Represents the "Japan-version Samhall" that SoE's Two-Axis Model ultimately aims to build — an organisation that simultaneously generates revenue and rehabilitates its workers.

### UK Remploy Closure and the Bridging Function Gap

Remploy, the UK's sheltered workshop system, was abolished in 2013 on the correct principle that segregation equals discrimination. However, the abolition simultaneously destroyed the **bridging function** — the structured transition pathway from support to competitive employment.

**Results**: Only 51% of former Remploy workers achieved competitive employment. 49% fell into a gap — and among people with severe disabilities, 80-90% became unemployed. The principle was right; the execution created a humanitarian crisis.

**Japan's paradox and SoE's proposal**: Japan is wrong to expand sheltered workshops (A-type and B-type), but Japan possesses something the UK lost — a comprehensive two-year bridging function (就労移行支援 / Vocational Transition Support). SoE proposes that Japan should eventually move away from sheltered workshops but strengthen the bridging function with Constitutional AI. For the UK, SoE offers "Vocational Rehabilitation 2.0" — Japan's bridging model enhanced with Constitutional Space and Input CAI — to fill the gap that Remploy's closure left behind.

This is a primary use case for SoE's international deployment and one of the reasons Susan Scott-Parker engaged with the framework.

### DEAI (Disability Ethical AI Alliance)

Founded by Susan Scott-Parker OBE. Key concepts adopted by SoE:
- **"Everyone Wins a Balloon"**: Inclusive evaluation philosophy — the goal is not to rank people but to ensure everyone receives what they need
- **Fit for Purpose**: Organisational readiness evaluation — is the organisation fit to serve the person, not is the person fit for the organisation
- **"Ammunition"**: Susan's term for research evidence — data that practitioners and advocates can use to persuade governments and service providers. SoE's NOCC data and forced labour framework provide this ammunition

## 9. The SROI Hypothesis: From 29.4:1 to a Two-Model Framework

### 9.1 The Original Hypothesis (29.4:1)

SoE initially proposed that for every unit invested in disability employment support, society receives 29.4 units of social return:

| Component | Hypothesised Value | Mechanism |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------|
| Economic output | 0.2 | Wages, tax revenue, reduced welfare payments |
| Self-esteem recovery | 5.0 | QOL improvement measurable via QALY; healthcare cost reduction through wellbeing |
| Anomie prevention | 15.0 | Prevention of "deaths of despair," social isolation escalation, and associated societal costs |
| Medical cost reduction | 5.0 | Reduced psychiatric hospitalisation, medication, and emergency service use |
| Criminal justice savings | 4.2 | Reduced incarceration, policing, and victim support costs |
| **Total** | **29.4** | |

### 9.2 Structural Critique and Methodological Refinement

External review identified four critical methodological issues with the 29.4:1 figure as a universal average:

**1. Base Rate Fallacy (確率的割引の欠如)**: The Anomie prevention component (15.0) and criminal justice savings (4.2) include the avoidance costs of extremely low-probability catastrophic events (e.g. mass violence). These are assigned as average deterministic values across all service users, without probability weighting. An expected value calculation — multiplying avoidance cost by risk probability per person — would dramatically reduce these figures for the general population.

**2. Time Axis and Discounting (時間軸の未設定)**: Self-esteem recovery (5.0) uses Japan's 1 QALY threshold (~5 million yen), but a single person can gain at most 1.0 QALY per year. Without specifying effect duration (5-10 years) and applying a social discount rate to calculate Net Present Value, the single-year figure is internally inconsistent.

**3. Double Counting (二重計上)**: Overlap exists between Anomie prevention (suicide/crime) and criminal justice savings, and between self-esteem recovery (mental health) and medical cost reduction. These must be restructured as MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) categories with explicit deduplication.

**4. Deadweight and Attribution (死荷重と帰属)**: The model assumes 100% of social value is attributable to the employment support programme. Natural recovery rates (deadweight) and contributions from other services — medical, educational, family — must be subtracted through attribution coefficients.

### 9.3 The Two-Model Restructuring

To resolve these issues while preserving the insight that SoE captures, the SROI framework is restructured into two distinct models:

#### Model A: General Employment Support (普遍的就労支援モデル)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target** | Typical vocational transition and continuing support users |
| **Expected SROI** | **1.5 – 6.0** |
| **Primary components** | Welfare-to-work transition, tax revenue generation, standard medical cost reduction |
| **Benchmark** | Aligned with UK supported employment programmes (SROI ~5.7:1) and international IPS studies |

#### Model B: Crisis Intervention Welfare (司法・危機介入福祉モデル)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target** | Persons with criminal justice involvement, long-term psychiatric hospitalisation, or documented high risk of self-harm/harm to others |
| **Expected SROI** | **15.0 – 29.4** |
| **Primary components** | Incarceration cost avoidance (~4.45 million yen/person/year), psychiatric hospitalisation reduction, catastrophic event prevention |
| **Justification** | For this population, the base rate is not negligible — the probability of re-offending, re-hospitalisation, or crisis is high and measurable. The Forced Labour Framework (Section 3.5) strengthens the case: the four vulnerability factors driving Anomie escalation (low self-esteem, information asymmetry, social isolation, absence of choice capacity) are structurally identical to ILO forced labour indicators |

### 9.4 Why This Matters

The two-model restructuring is not a retreat from the 29.4:1 figure — it is its academic operationalisation. The original hypothesis captured something real: that for certain high-risk individuals, the social cost of non-intervention is catastrophic. The refinement makes this claim defensible by specifying exactly who, under what conditions, and with what probability the extreme returns materialise.

This requires verification through existing data sources (Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare statistics; National Database of Health Insurance Claims; Suicide Prevention White Paper; Ministry of Justice recidivism data) and comparison with international SROI studies.
